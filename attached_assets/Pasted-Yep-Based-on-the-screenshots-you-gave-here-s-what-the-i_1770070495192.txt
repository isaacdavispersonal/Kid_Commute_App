## What the issue is

Those “white bars” at the **top** and **bottom** aren’t random iOS glitches — they’re coming from your **web layout intentionally adding safe-area space** using `env(safe-area-inset-*)`.

On iPhones with a notch / Dynamic Island / home indicator, iOS provides safe-area measurements:

* `env(safe-area-inset-top)` → height of the status bar/notch area
* `env(safe-area-inset-bottom)` → height needed to avoid the home indicator

Your app is using these values **as real layout spacing**, so when iOS reports a non-zero inset, you get visible “extra space” (the bars).

---

## Where it’s happening (top bar)

From your DOM inspector screenshot, this is the key element:

```html
<header class="border-b bg-card ...">
  <div class="h-[env(safe-area-inset-top,0px)]"></div>
  ...
</header>
```

### What that means:

* You have a **real empty `<div>`** inserted at the top of your header.
* Its height is set to:
  `env(safe-area-inset-top,0px)`
* When that value becomes something like **44px**, that div becomes 44px tall.

### Why it shows as a “white bar”:

* Your `<header>` uses `bg-card`, and in your theme that’s likely a **light/white surface**.
* So the empty spacer div becomes a visible white strip.

✅ **Location:** your **header component / app shell layout** (where the header is rendered).

---

## Why it appears “only after first login” then disappears after reopening

This is the biggest clue you gave.

That behavior happens when the safe-area value changes between renders:

* On first render (often login screen), the webview may report `safe-area-inset-top = 0`
* After auth/navigation and the app layout mounts, iOS recalculates and reports the correct inset (like 44px)
* The spacer div suddenly gains height → bar “appears”
* After a full app relaunch, the safe-area is known immediately, so the layout is stable and the effect doesn’t look as abrupt

So this is a **timing/layout transition issue** where the safe-area value is “settling” after the initial login navigation.

## Where it’s happening (bottom bar)

You mentioned this line (or similar) and that toggling it made the bottom bar disappear:

```css
html, body, #root {
  min-height: 100vh;
  min-height: 100svh;
  min-height: 100dvh;
}
```

This isn’t “wrong” by itself — but here’s what’s likely happening:

### What the bottom bar usually is in this setup

1. You have **padding** on your scrolling container (`<main>`) that uses `env(safe-area-inset-bottom)`

   * e.g. something like:

   ```tsx
   pb-[max(1.5rem,env(safe-area-inset-bottom))]
   ```
2. Or your scroll container ends before the full viewport height and the “rest” of the viewport shows the background of a different element (often `html`/`body`).
3. If any of those layers has a lighter background or border, you see a “bar” at the bottom.

Your note (“disabling/enabling this part made it disappear at the bottom”) suggests the **height definition is influencing which element is painting the bottom region**. When you toggle it, the viewport height recalculates and the bar shifts/disappears because a different layer is filling that space.

✅ **Likely location(s):**

* Your `<main>` container (because it uses bottom safe-area padding)
* Possibly a wrapper div with `overflow-hidden` + height constraints
* And the global CSS block for `html/body/#root` sizing (because it controls the overall painted region)
