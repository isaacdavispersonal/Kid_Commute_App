Route Flow Overview (Unified Driver Experience)
1. Route Setup (Admin Side)

Admin creates each route:

Adds all stops with names, addresses, and coordinates.

Assigns a driver and vehicle to that route.

Defines the order of stops and estimated times.

Once published:

The assigned driver can view that route from their app.

Only that driver can start, manage, and complete the route.

2. Before Route Starts

Before beginning:

The driver completes the Vehicle Inspection Checklist.

Once inspection is submitted, the Start Route button activates.

Live GPS tracking starts for parents to see how far the van is from their selected stop.

When Start Route is pressed:

The system opens the Route Dashboard — a single-page interface where the driver manages the entire trip.

From here, everything happens in one place:

The “stops list” appears with progress indicators.

A live counter shows:

“Van is X stops away” and “ETA: ___ minutes.”

3. During the Route (All in One Screen)

Once the route is active, the driver stays on the same page for all tasks.

Each stop appears in order with expandable cards or buttons like:

Stop 1: Oakwood Apartments — ETA 3 min
[Take Attendance] [Mark Stop Complete]


Driver Flow at Each Stop:

The driver arrives and selects the active stop.

Takes attendance (marks which students were dropped off/picked up). (Morning stops should have students listed as picked up, afternoon stop should have students listed as dropped off)

Taps Mark Stop as Complete once done.

When a stop is marked complete:

The stop turns green or collapses.

The next stop becomes active automatically.

The “X stops away” counter and ETAs update automatically for parents — no extra steps needed.

A parent notification is triggered:

“Your student’s stop has been completed.”

This continues until the final stop.

4. Finishing the Route

The last stop is considered the final stop in the route where drivers are returning the van, so no prompt should be needed for attendance or updates for parents

After the final stop is completed:

The screen automatically shows a prompt:
“All stops completed. Mark Route as Finished?”

When the driver taps Mark Route as Complete:

The session ends and is logged as Completed.

Attendance and timestamps are automatically saved.

Admins see the route marked as done in real time.

Parents see:

“Route Completed.”

5. Parent and Admin Visibility

Parents See:

Real-time van location and ETA to their stop.

“X stops away” counter that updates live.

Notifications when:

The route starts.

Their stop is completed.

The route ends.

Admins See:

All active and completed routes.

Live driver tracking and ETAs.

Attendance records per stop.

Start/end timestamps and route summary.

6. Summary of Driver-Side Simplicity

Everything the driver needs is on one continuous Route Page:

Start Route

Live ETAs and stop list

Attendance and stop completion buttons

Route completion prompt
