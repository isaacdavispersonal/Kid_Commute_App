## What the bottom white bar ACTUALLY is

### The culprit (confirmed by your inspector)

```css
html, body, #root {
  min-height: 100vh;
  min-height: 100svh;
  min-height: 100dvh;
}
```

### Why this creates the bottom bar

On iOS **inside a Capacitor WebView**:

* `100dvh` = *the full device viewport including areas **behind** the home indicator*
* But your **actual scroll container (`<main>`) does NOT extend into that area**
* Result:
  The **extra height** created by `min-height: 100dvh` gets painted by `html/body/#root`
* Since those elements have a background color → you see a **white bar at the bottom**

When you unchecked `min-height: 100dvh` in DevTools:
✅ The extra painted area disappeared
✅ The bottom bar vanished

That proves beyond doubt:

> **The bar is NOT content, NOT padding, NOT a bug — it’s an overextended root height.**

## Why this should NOT exist in your app

This rule is appropriate for:

* marketing websites
* full-page scrolling documents

It is **wrong** for:

* Capacitor / iOS apps
* apps with a **single internal scroll container**
* layouts where `html/body` are overflow-hidden (which yours are)

You already have:

* a controlled layout
* a single scroll container
* proper background handling

So this rule is doing **nothing useful** and actively breaking the UI.
