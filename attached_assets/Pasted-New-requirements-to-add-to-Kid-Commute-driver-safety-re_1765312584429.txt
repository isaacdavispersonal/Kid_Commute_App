New requirements to add to Kid Commute (driver safety, refresh UX, student info):

1. Driver Emergency Button & Flow

Screen: Driver dashboard (main screen drivers see after login).

We need an “Emergency” button that drivers can press when something serious happens.

Flow:

Driver taps Emergency.

App asks: “What kind of emergency is this?” with options like:

Behavior issue

Medical emergency

Vehicle issue

Other

After selection, show a verification step, e.g.:

“Are you sure you want to trigger an emergency notification?”

[Confirm] [Cancel]

After confirmation, ask who to contact:

Option A: Contact Admin → open the phone dialer with one of the admin phone numbers pre-filled.

Option B: Contact Emergency Services → open the phone dialer with 911 pre-filled.

Implementation hint: On mobile this should just use a standard tel: link to open the native phone app with the number filled in. We’re not actually placing the call inside our app; we just hand off to the dialer.

2. Refresh / Pull-to-Refresh Behavior

Our current dropdown/refresh interaction feels clunky and not like the native iOS pull-to-refresh.

Also, in some views, data is not actually refreshed, so users still have to close and reopen the app to see changes.

Expected behavior:

Use a smooth, native-style pull-to-refresh gesture on lists and key pages (especially routes, students, scheduler, etc.).

When pull-to-refresh is triggered, the app must:

Call the appropriate data refetch function(s).

Update the UI with fresh data.

Users should never need to force close the app just to see new info.

Treat this as both a UX improvement and a data refreshing reliability fix.

3. Student Medical Info & Badges

Each child should have medical info fields, editable by parents and admins, including:

Allergy information

Important medical notes (e.g. asthma, epilepsy, medication notes, etc.)

When any medical info exists for a child:

Assign a badge to that child (e.g. a small icon or label).

This badge should appear next to their name anywhere in the app, especially:

In route lists

In driver views

In student lists

When a driver or admin taps the student, they should be able to see the full medical info in the detail view.

4. Driver Student List Screen

Drivers need a Student section in their app that shows all students assigned to their route(s).

Behavior:

Show a list of all students assigned to that driver’s current route(s).

Each item should be tappable:

When tapped, show a student detail view with:

Basic info (name, pickup/dropoff, school, etc.)

Medical info if present (from section 3).

Medical badge clearly visible.

This should stay in sync with our existing logic where students are assigned to routes. The driver view is essentially a filtered view of students on that driver’s routes.
